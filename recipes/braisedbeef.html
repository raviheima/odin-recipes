Braised Beef

What is Braised Beef

Braising is a method of slowly cooking meat in a covered pot with liquid. This recipe braises chuck roast in beef broth and red wine.

This hearty dish offers a rich, earthy flavor that's perfect alongside mashed potatoes, buttered noodles, or crusty bread.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ½ pounds boneless beef chuck roast
  • 5 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 2 ½ teaspoons freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 pound cremini mushrooms, halved
  • 3 medium carrots, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 1 head garlic, cut in half lengthwise
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 3 sprigs thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire
  • 2 cups beef broth, or more as needed
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Preheat and Prep: Gather all ingredients and preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Season the Beef: Season beef pieces evenly on all sides with 3 ½ teaspoons salt, 2 teaspoons pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Sear the Beef: Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat. Add beef, reduce heat to medium-high and cook undisturbed until golden brown crust forms, about 4 minutes. Flip and brown all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. Cook the Vegetables: Add butter to the drippings. Once melted, add onion, mushrooms, and carrots. Cook 5 minutes, stirring often and scraping up browned bits. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  5. Deglaze and Combine: Stir in wine, scraping the pan. Return meat and juices to the pan. Add thyme, bay leaves, and Worcestershire.
  6. Add Broth: Pour in beef broth until it comes up 2/3 of the way up the beef (don't fully submerge). Bring to a simmer.
  7. Braise: Cover and place in preheated oven until meat is very tender, 2 to 2 ½ hours.
  8. Serve: Remove from oven, garnish with parsley, and enjoy.
